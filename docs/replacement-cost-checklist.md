# AC Replacement Cost Checklist for South Florida Homeowners

Use this checklist before signing any AC replacement contract.

## Before Getting Quotes

- [ ] Confirm your home's square footage (conditioned space only)
- [ ] Note the age and model of your current system
- [ ] List any comfort issues (rooms too hot/cold, humidity problems, uneven cooling)
- [ ] Check your last 12 months of electricity bills for usage trends
- [ ] Inspect visible ductwork for obvious damage, disconnections, or moisture
- [ ] Check if your electrical panel has capacity for a modern system
- [ ] Verify your home insurance covers AC equipment (some policies exclude old systems)

## During the Quote Process

- [ ] Request a Manual J load calculation (not a rule-of-thumb estimate)
- [ ] Ask for specific equipment model numbers, not just tonnage and SEER2
- [ ] Get itemized pricing: equipment, labor, materials, permits, disposal
- [ ] Confirm what is NOT included (ductwork repairs, electrical upgrades, drywall)
- [ ] Ask about manufacturer warranty AND installer labor warranty
- [ ] Verify the contractor is licensed and insured in your county
- [ ] Check the contractor's reviews and complaint history with the BBB
- [ ] Get at least three written quotes for comparison

## Comparing Quotes

- [ ] Compare equipment quality (brand, model tier, compressor type)
- [ ] Compare SEER2 ratings and calculate annual energy cost difference
- [ ] Compare warranty coverage (parts years + labor years)
- [ ] Check if ductwork assessment is included or extra
- [ ] Verify permit costs are included
- [ ] Ask about post-installation support and maintenance plans
- [ ] Calculate total cost of ownership over 10 and 15 years (purchase + energy + maintenance)

## Financing Review

- [ ] Read the full terms of any 0% promotional financing offer
- [ ] Understand what happens if the promotional period expires with a balance
- [ ] Compare contractor financing rates with your bank or credit union
- [ ] Check if PACE financing is available in your county
- [ ] Apply for FPL rebates before installation begins
- [ ] Keep all receipts and documentation for tax and insurance purposes

## After Installation

- [ ] Verify the system matches the quoted model numbers
- [ ] Confirm the permit was pulled and final inspection passed
- [ ] Register the manufacturer warranty (often requires online registration within 60 days)
- [ ] Save the installer's labor warranty documentation
- [ ] Schedule the first maintenance visit for 6 months after installation
- [ ] Monitor electricity bills for the expected efficiency improvement
- [ ] Set thermostat schedules optimized for your household routine
